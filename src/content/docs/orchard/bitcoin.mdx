Every mint sits on top of a Bitcoin node, and Orchard surfaces that node so you don't have
to drop to the command line to check on it. The Bitcoin view has two tabs — a **Dashboard**
overview and the **Oracle**, Orchard's sovereign price feed.

## Dashboard

A full node overview — info, hot wallet, and a live look at the mempool — is coming soon to
this tab. In the meantime, the [Home view](/orchard/home/) already summarises the
node: online status, peers, block height, chain weight, the on-chain balance backing your
mint, and current fee rates.

## Oracle

The Oracle tab is home to the **Sovereign USD Price Oracle** — a US-dollar price for bitcoin
derived from your own node's on-chain data, using the open-source
[UTXOracle](https://utxo.live/oracle/) method. No exchange APIs, no third-party feeds,
nothing that leaks what you're holding.

It shows the **latest price**, a chart of price history across a date range you choose, and
a **Backfill Prices** control to fill in history for any span back to 2020 — the progress
panel tracks how many days succeeded or failed as it works.

<Aside type="tip" title="Enable it first">
  The Oracle tab only appears once you've switched on the oracle under
  [Settings → Application → Bitcoin](/orchard/settings/#bitcoin). Off by default, it then
  derives the previous day's price once daily at 00:00 UTC.
</Aside>
