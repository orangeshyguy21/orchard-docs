Home is the screen Orchard opens to — the single view where the state of your mint comes
together. Instead of querying each service by hand, you see Bitcoin, Lightning, and the mint
summarised one below the other, each block appearing once that service is configured.

<Aside type="tip" title="Tend, don't babysit">
  The point of the Home view is to make the healthy case boring: a quick look tells you the
  mint is fine, and anything that needs attention stands out.
</Aside>

## Bitcoin

An **Info** card covers the node — online status, connected peers, block height, and chain
weight — and a **Hot Wallet** card shows the on-chain balance backing your mint, with live
mempool fee rates alongside. Open it for the full [Bitcoin view](/orchard/bitcoin/).

## Lightning

An **Info** card shows the node, its online status, peers, and open channels, and a
**Channel Summary** totals your capacity and splits it into local and remote. Open it for
the full [Lightning view](/orchard/lightning/).

## Mint

An **Info** card shows the mint name, status, and description; an **Activity** card tracks
operations and volume over a day, week, month, or year — mints, melts, and swaps, with
completion rates; and a **Balance Sheet** weighs assets against liabilities per unit, with
liability coverage and fee revenue. Open it for the full [Mint view](/orchard/mint/).

## Ecash

Ecash has a reserved spot on the Home view — a wallet view is coming soon.

<Aside type="note" title="Health runs deeper than the mint">
  A mint is only as healthy as the layers under it. A problem at the
  [Lightning](/orchard/lightning/) or [Bitcoin](/orchard/bitcoin/) layer shows up as mint
  trouble — keep those backends in view too.
</Aside>
