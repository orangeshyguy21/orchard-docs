Running a mint well means noticing when something needs attention — and not having
to stare at a terminal to do it. Orchard surfaces the mint's health and activity so
the signal finds you.

## What to watch

- **Health** — whether the mint and the backends it depends on are reachable and
  responding.
- **Activity** — mint and melt operations as they happen, so volume and anomalies
  are visible.

<Aside type="note" title="Health runs deeper than the mint">
  A mint is only as healthy as the layers under it. A problem at the Lightning or
  Bitcoin layer shows up as mint trouble — keep those backends in view too.
</Aside>

<Aside type="caution" title="Alerting details are on the way">
  How Orchard notifies you — and which channels it supports — is still taking shape,
  and lands here as it settles. The
  [Orchard repository](https://github.com/cashubtc/orchard) has the source code in
  the meantime.
</Aside>
