The mint itself — the service that issues and redeems Chaumian ecash against your
Lightning node. This is the piece Orchard will manage, so once it's running and
settling through Lightning, you're one step from the dashboard.

## Pick an implementation

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Nutshell">
    [Nutshell](https://github.com/cashubtc/nutshell) — the reference Cashu mint and
    wallet in Python. The quickest way to a working mint and a natural fit for
    Orchard to manage.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="CDK">
    [CDK](https://github.com/cashubtc/cdk) — the Cashu Development Kit in Rust,
    whose `cdk-mintd` runs a production-minded mint daemon.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## What you'll set up

- **The mint**, configured to settle against your Lightning node.
- **Mint info** — name, description, and contact, shown to wallets that connect.
- **Persistent storage and backups** for the mint's keys and database.

<Aside type="caution" title="Exact commands are on the way">
  Until the step-by-step setup lands here, follow your chosen implementation's docs
  — [Nutshell](https://github.com/cashubtc/nutshell) or
  [CDK](https://github.com/cashubtc/cdk) — as the source of truth for running a
  mint.
</Aside>
