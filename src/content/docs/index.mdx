## Who it's for

Orchard is for people who want to run their **own** mint instead of trusting
someone else's:

- **Sovereign individuals** — you'd rather operate the mint than rely on a custodian.
- **Self-hosters** — you're comfortable keeping a node and a server online.
- **Communities and projects** — you want to issue ecash for your people, on
  infrastructure you control.

You don't have to be a Lightning wizard to start — just willing to tend your own
setup. Orchard handles the day-to-day, so the bar is "run a server," not "live in
a terminal."

<Aside type="tip" title="Brand new?">
  Cashu is private, instant, low-fee digital cash with no accounts and no
  surveillance. If that's unfamiliar, skim [cashu.space](https://cashu.space)
  first; the rest of these docs assume that mental model.
</Aside>

## What Orchard does

A Cashu mint issues and redeems ecash backed by a Lightning wallet. Running one
well means watching balances, keys, and proofs; reacting when something needs
attention; and doing it without babysitting a terminal. Orchard wraps that work
in one manager:

- **Operate your mint** — keys, balances, and mint info from a single dashboard.
- **Stay informed** — health and activity surfaced where you already are.
- **Tend, don't babysit** — the groundskeeper watches the mint so you don't have
  to refresh a log all day.

## What you'll need

<Steps>

1. **A Cashu mint to manage.** Orchard manages a mint — it doesn't replace one.
   You'll point it at your mint's backend.

2. **A Lightning backend.** Cashu mints settle against Lightning, so you'll need
   a node or service your mint can reach.

3. **Somewhere to run it.** Orchard is self-hosted: a VPS, a home server, or any
   box you control and can keep online.

</Steps>
