Cashu mints settle ecash against Lightning, so the mint points at a Lightning
backend you run. If you already operate a node, this path connects it rather than
standing up a new one. How you wire it depends on which implementation you run.

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="LND">
    [LND](https://github.com/lightningnetwork/lnd) connects via its gRPC/REST API,
    authenticated with a macaroon and TLS certificate. Your mint uses those
    credentials to open and settle invoices.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="Core Lightning">
    [Core Lightning](https://github.com/ElementsProject/lightning) (CLN) connects
    over its RPC socket — or a plugin such as `cln-grpc` — which your mint uses to
    open and settle invoices.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## What to have ready

- **The node's address** and API port, reachable from where your mint runs.
- **Credentials** — macaroon and TLS cert for LND, or the RPC socket / gRPC
  details for CLN.
- **Inbound and outbound liquidity** appropriate to the volume you expect to mint
  and melt.

<Aside type="caution" title="Exact connection steps are on the way">
  Until the per-node settings land here, your mint implementation's documentation
  is the source of truth for which Lightning backends it supports and how to
  authenticate against them.
</Aside>
