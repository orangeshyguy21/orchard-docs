Already running [Bitcoin Core](https://bitcoincore.org)? Good — that's the base
layer done. Nothing in this path replaces your node; the job is making sure your
Lightning backend can reach it, since that's what your mint settles through.

## What to check

- **RPC access** — your Lightning node connects to `bitcoind` over RPC (and
  usually a notification channel for new blocks and transactions). Confirm those
  are enabled and reachable from wherever Lightning runs.
- **Index requirements** — some Lightning backends expect a transaction index or
  an unpruned chain. Check your Lightning node's docs before assuming a pruned node
  will do.
- **It's synced** — Lightning can't operate against a node still catching up.

<Aside type="note" title="Bitcoin Core connects through Lightning">
  Orchard talks to your mint, and your mint talks to Lightning — Lightning is what
  holds the connection to Bitcoin Core. So "connecting Bitcoin" here means pointing
  your Lightning node at the right endpoints.
</Aside>

  [Bitcoin Core documentation](https://bitcoincore.org/en/doc/) and your Lightning
  backend's guide for wiring the two together.
</Aside>
